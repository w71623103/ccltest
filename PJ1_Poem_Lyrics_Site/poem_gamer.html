A Gamer

Yinqi Wang

I am a person who is the same as everyone else;
A Homo Sapiens who lives alone.
Seven feet height,
Two hundred pounds weight.
I can walk, I can talk, I can eat.
I can laugh, I can cry, I can shout.
But my shouting is always missed,
For the one who heard never cared.
“You’re just a ‘one’ who differs from the ‘mass’.”
“Who cares about your interest, your doubt, or your rage?”
Rage, rage!
But rage is the ending on my stage.
After raging, what can I change?
Hum,
I guess only the minutes that was added on my age.

~Welcome to Game! ~

I am a person who is not myself.
A Homo Ludens who sits in my realm.
A magic circle distinguishes my state,
When sitting inside, I am on my game.
In the game, I am no more a No One.
I am the center under the dome;
I am the hero who fights the doom.
I can run, I can jump, I can fly.
I can build, I can break, I can shout.
In the game, my shouting won’t be missed.
For the sight of the creatures in game are fixed
Precisely on my Avatar.
Outside the game, they curse me as a “NEET”;
Inside the game, they praise me as a “Hero”.

I’m a gamer, a sick Gamer.
A Gamer who dislikes real life.
The game gives me a shelter.
Entering the shelter, I become anyone I want.
And do anything I want.
